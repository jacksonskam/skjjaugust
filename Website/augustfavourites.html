movies

La Haine (1993)

  • directed by Mathieu Kassovitz
  • language: french
  • i first saw this film in a french film class i had in high school and was absolutely blown away. to begin, this film is beautiful. every single shot looks as if it could be put in a museum and the artistic choices incredibly assist in conveying the film's themes. La Haine is also a stellar political commentary. being set around the Paris riots of the mid 90s, it delves into topics like police brutality against minority communities, islamaphobia, anti-semitism, , and it even passes the socialist test by demonstrating class consciousness through this group of jewish, a black, and algerian young men having more in common with each other than with the upper class Parisians whom they run into throughout the film.
